Pão de Queijo

Description

Pão de queijo is a traditional Brazilian cheese bread that is made with tapioca flour and cheese. It is a popular snack in Brazil and is often enjoyed with coffee or as an appetizer. Pão de queijo is known for its chewy texture and cheesy flavor, and it is a favorite among locals and tourists alike.

  • Preparation time: 30 minutes
  • Cooking time: 2 hours
  • Servings: 6

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of milk
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 cups of tapioca flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup of grated cheese

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the milk, oil, and salt and bring to a boil.
  3. Remove from heat and add the tapioca flour, stirring until well combined.
  4. Let the mixture cool slightly, then add the eggs and cheese, stirring until well combined.
  5. Using a spoon, drop the dough onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the pão de queijo is golden brown and puffed up.
  7. Remove from the oven and let cool slightly before serving.
  8. Enjoy!
